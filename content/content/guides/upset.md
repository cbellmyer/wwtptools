<div id="overview" class="story-chapter-label">[ Overview ]</div>

Process upsets fall into two categories: **hydraulic** (flow-driven) and **biological** (chemistry-driven). Identifying which you're dealing with within the first 15 minutes determines whether you have hours or days to correct it.

The triage question is simple: did something change with flow, or did something change with the mixed liquor? Your answer shapes every action that follows.

<div id="hydraulic" class="story-chapter-label">[ Hydraulic Upsets ]</div>

Hydraulic upsets — wet weather events, influent pump failures, clarifier short-circuiting — respond to operational adjustments within one hydraulic retention time. The biology hasn't changed; only the flow conditions around it have.

Common indicators: sudden SVI spike, blanket rise in secondary clarifiers without change in MLSS, effluent turbidity increase without change in F/M.

<div id="biological" class="story-chapter-label">[ Biological Upsets ]</div>

Biological upsets — toxic industrial loads, sudden temperature swings, nutrient imbalances — can take days or weeks to recover from. The organisms themselves are stressed or dying; operational adjustments buy time but don't substitute for biomass recovery.

Early warning signs: DO demand dropping despite no load change (toxic), VSS:TSS ratio shifting, filamentous growth in microscopy, alkalinity declining faster than expected.
